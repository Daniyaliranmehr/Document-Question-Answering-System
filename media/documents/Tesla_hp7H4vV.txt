Nikola Tesla was one of the most influential inventors and electrical engineers in history. He is best known for his pioneering work on alternating current (AC) electrical systems, which became the global standard for electricity transmission. Tesla was born in 1856 in what is now Croatia and later moved to the United States, where he worked with major figures in science and engineering, including Thomas Edison. His ideas and inventions laid the foundation for many modern electrical technologies.

Throughout his career, Tesla developed numerous groundbreaking concepts and devices, such as the Tesla coil, early wireless communication systems, and innovations in electromagnetism. He was deeply interested in the idea of wireless energy transmission and dreamed of providing free electricity to the world. Although many of his projects were not fully realized during his lifetime due to financial and technical limitations, his research significantly advanced the understanding of electrical engineering.

Today, Tesla is widely recognized as a visionary whose work was ahead of its time. Many of his concepts are now applied in modern technologies such as radio systems, wireless communication, and power distribution networks. In recent years, his legacy has gained even more attention, symbolizing creativity, innovation, and scientific curiosity. His contributions continue to inspire engineers and scientists around the world.
